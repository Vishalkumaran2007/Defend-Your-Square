# Defend Your Space 

**Defend Your Space** is a basic third-person shooting survival game developed during the **Gameora 24-Hour Hackathon** conducted by Sri Sakthi Institute of Engineering and Technology. The project was created within a limited time frame with the goal of building a fully playable game prototype while experimenting with game mechanics and rapid development tools.

The core idea of the game is simple: the player must defend a central square zone from waves of enemies that continuously spawn around the arena. As the enemies approach from multiple directions, the player must move strategically, aim carefully, and eliminate threats before they reach and overwhelm the defense area.

---

##  Game Concept

The gameplay revolves around a simple but engaging **defense and survival loop**. The player acts as a protector of a square zone located at the center of the arena. Enemies spawn from different positions and gradually increase in number as the game progresses.

The player must survive for as long as possible while preventing enemies from taking over the square. Each wave introduces more enemies and increases the overall challenge, encouraging players to improve their movement and shooting strategy.

---

##  Gameplay Mechanics

The game focuses on fast and simple mechanics that allow players to quickly understand and engage with the gameplay.

- The player can move freely around the arena.
- Enemies spawn periodically in waves.
- The player must shoot enemies to eliminate them.
- Each wave increases the number of enemies and the overall difficulty.
- The game continues until the player is defeated or overwhelmed.

##  Technology Used

This game was developed using **GDevelop**, an event-based game development platform that allows developers to build games without traditional programming.

Key advantages of using GDevelop in this project included:

- Rapid prototyping of gameplay mechanics  
- Visual event-based logic system  
- Quick implementation of player movement and shooting mechanics  
- Efficient development within the 24-hour hackathon timeframe
